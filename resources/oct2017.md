## Why It Matters

OCT2017 still functions as the default sanity-check benchmark for OCT classification and remains useful for baseline comparisons and pretraining.

## Highlights

- Still the classic public OCT classification baseline used across many papers.
- Large enough to serve as a pretraining or sanity-check benchmark.

## Caveats

- Different papers sometimes report slightly different file counts depending on filtering or release interpretation.
- It is strong as a baseline, but much narrower in disease diversity than newer OCT datasets.
